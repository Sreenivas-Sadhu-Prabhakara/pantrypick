Questions

How do I find recipes with ingredients I already have?

Type the ingredients in your kitchen into the pantry box on the left. pantrypick matches them against a library of about 150 everyday recipes and ranks the results by how much you already have — recipes you can cook right now appear first, then recipes missing one ingredient, then two, and so on. Each recipe shows exactly which ingredients you are still missing.

Does this work offline?

Yes. Everything runs in your browser. The recipe library is bundled into the page, all matching happens on your device, and nothing is ever sent to a server. Once the page has loaded you can turn off your connection and it keeps working. Your pantry is saved locally in your browser, not in any account.

Does pantrypick invent new recipes with AI?

No. This is a deliberately hand-picked library of about 150 real, everyday recipes with tested-feeling quantities and steps. It does not generate recipes on the fly. That is the point: the results are honest, repeatable, and work with no connection.

What are staples and why are they assumed?

Staples are the everyday basics most kitchens always have — salt, pepper, water, oil, sugar, flour, butter, garlic and onion. pantrypick assumes you have them so you do not have to type them every time. If you are actually out of one, untick it in the Staples list and recipes that need it will count it as missing.

It knows scallion is green onion?

Yes. pantrypick normalises common ingredient names across regions, so scallion and green onion, coriander and cilantro, aubergine and eggplant, capsicum and bell pepper, courgette and zucchini, and many more all match the same thing.

Can I trust the vegetarian, vegan or gluten-free tags?

Treat them as a helpful best-effort starting point, not a guarantee. The tags are applied by hand and checked against each ingredient list, but products vary and cross-contamination happens. Always read the ingredients yourself if you cook for allergies or a strict diet.
